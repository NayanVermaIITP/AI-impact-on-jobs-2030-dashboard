# AI Impact on Jobs 2030 Dashboard

## Overview
The rapid growth of Artificial Intelligence is reshaping industries and changing workforce demands. This project analyzes how AI and automation may affect different job roles by 2030 through an interactive Excel dashboard.

The dashboard provides insights into automation probability, AI exposure levels, salary trends, educational requirements, and occupational risk categories, helping users understand future employment trends and workforce transformation.

## Business Problem
As AI adoption increases, organizations and professionals need to identify which jobs are most vulnerable to automation and what skills or education levels may remain valuable in the future.

This analysis aims to answer:
- Which jobs have the highest automation risk?
- How does AI exposure vary across occupations?
- What is the relationship between salary and education level?
- Which workforce segments are most affected by AI-driven changes?

## Dataset Features
The dataset includes information such as:
- Job Titles
- Education Levels
- Average Salary
- AI Exposure Index
- Automation Probability (2030)
- Risk Categories

## Data Cleaning & Preparation
The following preprocessing steps were performed:
- Removed duplicate records
- Checked for missing values
- Standardized categorical variables
- Cleaned inconsistent entries
- Prepared data for dashboard analysis

## Dashboard Features
- Average Salary by Education Level
- Job Titles with Highest Automation Probability
- Distribution of Risk Categories
- AI Exposure Index vs Automation Probability
- Interactive Slicers and Filters

## Key Insights
- Several occupations show a high likelihood of automation by 2030.
- Higher educational qualifications are generally associated with higher average salaries.
- Jobs with greater AI exposure tend to demonstrate higher automation probabilities.
- Risk categories help identify occupations that may require reskilling or adaptation in the future.
